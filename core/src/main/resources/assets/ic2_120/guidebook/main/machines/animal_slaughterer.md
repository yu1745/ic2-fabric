# Animal Slaughterer

<BlockImage id="ic2_120:animal_slaughterer" p:facing="north" scale="4" />

The Animal Slaughterer keeps a crowded pen trimmed down automatically. Once enough managed animals are inside its scan range, it removes the surplus animals and stores their simulated drops in its internal output grid.

## Energy and Storage

- **EU Storage**: 10,000 EU
- **Tier**: 1 (LV, 32 EU/packet)
- **Input rate**: 32 EU/t by default
- **Inventory**: 15 output slots, 4 upgrade slots, 1 battery slot, and 1 shears slot
- **Fluids**: none

## Operation

Every second, the machine scans a box expanded 4 blocks from itself, covering a **9x9x9** area centered on the block. It counts managed passive animals: pigs, cows, mooshrooms, sheep, chickens, rabbits, horses, donkeys, mules, and llamas.

The machine does nothing below 32 animals. At 32 or more animals, it tries to slaughter the surplus until only 16 remain. The current implementation counts all managed animals in range and does not check animal age, so keep breeding pens arranged so young animals are not in the slaughterer's range if you want to protect them.

The slaughterer must have shears in its shears slot. One point of shears durability is consumed per animal slaughtered; when the shears break, the run stops. The machine also requires enough stored EU to pay the scan cost before starting: **1 EU per animal counted**. Each generated drop stack then costs **20 EU**.

Drops are generated by the machine's own table rather than by vanilla loot tables, so player kills, Looting, experience, fire-aspect cooking, and entity-specific loot table hooks are not involved. Supported drops include porkchop, beef, leather, mushrooms from mooshrooms, mutton, wool from unsheared sheep, chicken, feathers, rabbit drops, and leather from horses, donkeys, mules, and llamas. If the output grid cannot accept a generated stack, the leftover stack is dropped at the machine.

## Usage

Place the Animal Slaughterer so its 9x9x9 scan box overlaps the pen you want to control. Supply EU from any side or place a charged battery in the battery slot, and keep shears stocked before expecting any slaughtering to occur.

Use it with an Animal-Matron by letting the matron raise the population while the slaughterer trims the same pen once it reaches 32 animals. Because the slaughterer keeps the population near 16, leave enough space and food automation for the herd to rebuild between runs.

## Upgrades and Automation

Supported upgrades are <ItemLink id="ic2_120:energy_storage_upgrade" />, <ItemLink id="ic2_120:transformer_upgrade" />, and <ItemLink id="ic2_120:ejector_upgrade" />. Each energy storage upgrade adds 10,000 EU capacity. Transformer upgrades raise the accepted voltage tier and input limit. Ejector upgrades can push items from the 15 output slots into adjacent inventories, using their configured filter and side.

Item automation can insert upgrades into upgrade slots, batteries into the battery slot, and shears into the shears slot. The output slots are not valid insertion targets. Automation can extract from the output slots, upgrade slots, battery slot, and shears slot, so filter external pipes if you do not want them to pull tools or upgrades back out.
