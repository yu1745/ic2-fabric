# Fluids, Cells, and Buckets

IC2 uses buckets, cells, tanks, and pipes to move fluids. Cells work well in recipes and inventory slots, buckets interact nicely with vanilla mechanics, and tanks and pipes are the backbone of automation.

## Common Fluids

| Fluid | Common Use |
|------|------------|
| Coolant / Hot Coolant | Nuclear reactor fluid cooling, heat exchange |
| Steam / Superheated Steam | Steam Generator, Steam Kinetic Generator |
| Biomass / Biofuel | Fermenter and Semifluid Generator |
| UUMatter | Replicator system |
| Distilled Water | Advanced thermal chains |
| Construction Foam | Construction Foam Sprayer, foam blocks |
| Weed-EX | Crop care |
| Creosote, Compressed Air, Pahoehoe Lava | Auxiliary fluids for steam, Coke Oven, and tool chains |

## Cells

- Empty Cells can hold water, lava, coolant, hot coolant, steam, superheated steam, biomass, biofuel, UUMatter, Weed-EX, Construction Foam, and more.
- Compressed Air Cells can be consumed by the Hazmat Helmet underwater to restore oxygen.
- Reactor Coolant Cells are not regular fluid cells; they are internal heat-buffer components in the reactor.

## Tanks and Pipes

- Bronze, Iron, Steel, and Iridium tanks are used for fixed fluid storage.
- Bronze Pipes and Carbon Fibre Pipes form fluid networks.
- The Pump Attachment lets pipes draw fluid from adjacent containers or world blocks.
