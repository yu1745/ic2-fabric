# Nuclear Reactor (Electric Mode)

<BlockImage id="ic2_120:nuclear_reactor" scale="4" />

The Nuclear Reactor is a powerful end-game EU generator that produces energy through controlled nuclear fission. It forms a multiblock structure and requires careful heat management to operate safely.

This page covers the **Electric Mode** (dry, EU-output) setup. For the fluid-cooled **Fluid Mode** (pressure-vessel shell, fluid ports, HU output), see [Fluid-Cooled Reactor](nuclear_reactor_fluid_mode.md).

## Multiblock Structure

The Nuclear Reactor multiblock is composed of the following components:

- **Nuclear Reactor**: The core of the structure, which holds fuel rods and components in a 3x3 or 6x9 grid (when reactor chambers are attached).
- **Reactor Chamber**: Attaches to the sides of the Reactor (up to 6 chambers, one per face). Each chamber expands the internal component grid, allowing more fuel rods, cooling cells, and heat exchangers.

The **Reactor Access Hatch**, **Reactor Fluid Port**, **Reactor Redstone Port**, and **Reactor Pressure Vessel** are covered on the [Fluid-Cooled Reactor](nuclear_reactor_fluid_mode.md) page — they are shared by both modes but are documented there since they define the fluid-cooled Fluid Mode shell.

## Power Generation

### Core Formula

```
base pulse = 1 + cells / 2
neighbor pulses = number of adjacent fuel rods + reflectors (each contributes 1)
total pulse = (base pulse + neighbor pulses) × cells
EU/t = total pulse × 5
```

`cells` is the rod's cell count (single=1, dual=2, quad=4). Each orthogonally adjacent fuel rod or neutron reflector adds 1 pulse. The reactor processes fuel rods once per second (20 ticks); the per-second total (`total pulse × 100`) is released evenly, giving `total pulse × 5` EU/t.

**Note:** A neighbor pulse is counted per **occupied slot**, not per cell. An adjacent single, dual, or quad fuel rod each contributes exactly **+1 pulse** — a quad rod next door does not add 4.

| Fuel Rod Type | Base EU/t (no adjacency) |
|----------|---:|
| Single Uranium Fuel Rod | 5 EU/t |
| Dual Uranium Fuel Rod | 20 EU/t |
| Quad Uranium Fuel Rod | 60 EU/t |

### MOX Fuel Rods

MOX output scales with reactor temperature (`heat / maxHeat`):

```
multiplier = 4 × (heat / maxHeat) + 1
EU/t = total pulse × multiplier × 5
```

| Reactor Temp | Multiplier | Single MOX EU/t (no adjacency) |
|---:|---:|---:|
| 0 | ×1 | 5 |
| 2,500 | ×2 | 10 |
| 5,000 | ×3 | 15 |
| 7,500 | ×4 | 20 |
| 10,000 | ×5 | 25 |

## Heat Management

The reactor's base heat capacity is **10,000 HU**. If the heat exceeds this limit, the reactor will explode in a catastrophic nuclear meltdown. Heat is generated by fuel rods during operation and must be managed through:

- **Cooling Cells**: Absorb heat from adjacent fuel rods and dissipate it over time.
- **Heat Exchangers**: Redistribute heat between components to balance thermal load.
- **Component Heat Vents**: Dissipate heat directly from adjacent components.
- **Overclocked Heat Vents**: Dissipate heat more aggressively.

### Reactor Plating

Installing **Reactor Plating** or **Containment Reactor Plating** in the grid raises the maximum heat capacity and reduces explosion power. Each plating stacks additively for heat capacity and multiplicatively for explosion power:

| Component | Max Heat Bonus | Explosion Power |
|------|---:|---:|
| Reactor Plating | +500 HU | ×0.9 |
| Containment Reactor Plating | +1,700 HU | ×0.99 |

### Temperature Thresholds

| Reactor Temp | Environmental Effect |
|---:|---|
| &gt; 4,000 | Random blocks within 5×5×5 may catch fire |
| &gt; 5,000 | Random water within 5×5×5 may evaporate |
| &gt; 7,000 | Entities within 7×7×7 take nuclear heat damage |
| &gt; 8,500 | Random blocks within 5×5×5 may turn to lava |
| ≥ 10,000 | **Nuclear reactor explosion** |

A Hazmat Suit makes you immune to the **nuclear heat damage** above 7,000 (this is heat damage, not radiation).

For the full numerical values of every fuel rod, vent, exchanger, and plating, see [Nuclear Blocks and Components](../reference/nuclear_components.md).

## Output

The reactor outputs EU through its core block. Use HV or EV cables to connect the reactor to your energy grid. The reactor automatically distributes EU to connected cables, storage, and machines.
