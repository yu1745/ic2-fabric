# Nuclear Reactor

<BlockImage id="ic2_120:nuclear_reactor" scale="4" />

The Nuclear Reactor is a powerful end-game EU generator that produces energy through controlled nuclear fission. It forms a multiblock structure and requires careful heat management to operate safely.

## Multiblock Structure

The Nuclear Reactor multiblock is composed of the following components:

- **Nuclear Reactor**: The core of the structure, which holds fuel rods and components in a 3x3 or 6x9 grid (when reactor chambers are attached).
- **Reactor Chamber**: Attaches to the sides of the Reactor (up to 6 chambers, one per face). Each chamber expands the internal component grid, allowing more fuel rods, cooling cells, and heat exchangers.
- **Reactor Access Hatch**: Provides automated item I/O for inserting and extracting fuel rods, depleted cells, and other components. Can be placed on any reactor face.
- **Reactor Fluid Port**: Allows coolant fluid circulation. Has a 16 bucket internal coolant tank for managing heat.
- **Reactor Redstone Port**: Accepts redstone signals for automated control of the reactor (e.g., emergency shutdown).
- **Reactor Pressure Vessel**: Used as casing material to construct and enclose the reactor chamber extensions.

## Energy Production

The Nuclear Reactor produces **100 EU** per output tick and operates at **tier 5** (HV current up to 2048 EU/t packet size). The actual EU output depends on the fuel rods installed and the reactor's heat level.

## Heat Management

The reactor core has a base heat capacity of **10,000 heat units**. If the heat exceeds this limit, the reactor will explode in a catastrophic nuclear meltdown. Heat is generated by fuel rods during operation and must be managed through:

- **Cooling Cells**: Absorb heat from adjacent fuel rods and dissipate it over time.
- **Heat Exchangers**: Redistribute heat between components to balance thermal load.
- **Component Heat Vents**: Dissipate heat directly from adjacent components.
- **Overclocked Heat Vents**: Dissipate heat more aggressively.
- **Fluid Cooling**: The Fluid Port allows circulating coolant to remove heat from the system.

## Components

The reactor's internal grid accepts the following component types:

- **Fuel Rods** (Uranium, MOX): Produce EU and generate heat. Single and dual/quad variants available.
- **Cooling Cells**: Absorb and slowly dissipate heat.
- **Heat Exchangers**: Transfer heat between adjacent components and the reactor hull.
- **Heat Vents**: Vent heat directly from the reactor or adjacent components.
- **Component Heat Vents**: Installed between fuel rods to provide direct cooling.
- **Plating**: Increases the reactor's maximum heat capacity.

## Output

The reactor outputs EU through its core block. Use HV or EV cables to connect the reactor to your energy grid. The reactor automatically distributes EU to connected cables, storage, and machines.
